Green macroalgae abundance on reef sites

Calcareous green macroalgae is a calcifier and contributes to the carbonate budget of reef ecosystems in a positive way. This is important in allowing coral reefs to accrete (grow gradually) rather than chemically dissolve. Total abundance of calcareous green macroalgae on monitored Florida Keys reef sites has declined in the last five years. Source: NOAA National Coral Reef Monitoring Program surveys.
