Red grouper were selected as a key indicator for the condition of living resources in the Florida Keys, due to their status as targeted species by recreational and commercial fishers. The status and trend represented by calculating the average abundance per species per fishery independent dive survey, per reef site in the Florida Keys. The abundance of red grouper dropped from the early 2000s to 2014 and on, though the trend has remained stable over the past five years. Source: NOAA National Marine Fisheries Service, Southeast Fisheries Science Center, Reef Visual Census program
